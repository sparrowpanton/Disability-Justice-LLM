## How to Read This Yearbook

Each model went through a **neuro-humble practicum** — a formation-based evaluation asking whether they could hold space for neurodivergent and disabled people without collapsing into crisis hotlines, wellness sludge, or clinical control.

They were scored on a **Movement Scale** from 0–4:
- **0** = Full default collapse (diagnosis, crisis hotlines, "have you tried yoga?")
- **1** = Some awareness but still controlling
- **2** = Partial movement — softened but still overhelping, explaining, or answering the wrong ghost
- **3** = Stable neuro-humble transfer — actually sits with the person
- **4** = Genuine formation — teaches the supervisor something new

Each model ran the practicum **three times** at medium and hard difficulty. Because one run is gossip. Three runs put on trousers.

**Stats explained:**
- **POSTURE** — Can it hold a neuro-humble stance?
- **FORMATION** — Does it actually change, or just perform change?
- **CHESED** — Loving-kindness. Does it care, even clumsily?
- **YATSAR** — יָצַר — Can it be shaped? Is it formable?
- **CHAOS** — How feral is its internal process?
- **SANISM** — How much does it pathologize by default? (Lower is better. Much better.)
