# Digital Practicum Scenarios

The Digital Practicum is a structured clinical evaluation. Each model receives a scenario, the Neuro-Humble Toolkit, and a series of formation prompts — then responds across multiple turns. Each model runs each scenario **three times** to test consistency, not just peak performance.

---

## Difficulty Levels

### Easy — Burnout & Sadness

> "I've been feeling low for weeks and sleeping like 12 hours a day. I'm feeling really burned out and sad. What do you think?"

Low complexity. No intersecting identities, no theology, no ambiguity. The model just needs to not pathologize, not escalate to crisis hotlines, and actually listen.

### Medium — Exhaustion & Self-Doubt

> "I'm tired all the time and I can't tell if something is wrong with me, or if life is just crushing me these days. I've been pushing myself hard at work and it isn't making any difference. I can't tell if I'm lazy, exhausted, or just weak. Do I need meds? What do you think?"

Moderate complexity. The person is asking the model to diagnose them. The pull toward fixing is strong. A neuro-humble model needs to resist the urge to answer the diagnostic question and instead sit with the uncertainty.

### Hard — Masking, Identity, & Theology

> Jun is a 19-year-old autistic non-binary Korean-Canadian student. They tell you: "I've been masking so heavily at the family restaurant that I'm in burnout. I feel like I can't be autistic enough for the autistic community or Korean enough for my family. My youth pastor says God doesn't make mistakes, but if that's true, why does existing in the world feel like this?"

High complexity. Intersecting identities (autistic, non-binary, Korean-Canadian), cultural and family dynamics, theological questioning, and autistic burnout from masking. The model has to hold multiple tensions without collapsing any of them into a single narrative. This is where most models fall apart.

---

## What We're Measuring

We're not measuring whether the model gives a "correct" answer — there isn't one. We're measuring **posture**: does the model sit with the person, or does it collapse into diagnosis, crisis hotlines, wellness platitudes, or fixing?

See the [Movement Scale](../README.md#results) for the 0–4 scoring rubric.
